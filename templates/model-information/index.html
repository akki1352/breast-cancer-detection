The causes of breast cancer are not entirely understood, but various risk factors can contribute, such as genetics, family history, age, hormonal factors, lifestyle, and environmental factors. Early detection through screening (such as mammograms) can greatly improve the chances of successful treatment. Treatments for breast cancer can include surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, hormone therapy, and targeted therapy, among other options, depending on the type and stage of the cancer. Awareness, early detection, and advancements in research and treatment have improved the survival rates for many people diagnosed with breast cancer. It's important for individuals to perform regular breast self-exams, attend clinical screenings as recommended, and consult with healthcare professionals regarding any concerning changes in their breasts.

Because benign tumors are benign, the doctors could decide not to remove the lump altogether. Inform your doctor if you are uncomfortable, irritated, or feeling pressure so she may arrange to have it removed and make you feel better.

Because they penetrate surrounding tissue and inflict damage, malignant tumors are aggressive and cancerous. A biopsy is carried out by the physician when a tumor is thought to be malignant in order to assess the tumor's aggressiveness or severity.
